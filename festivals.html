Lohri

(Punjab): Lohri is a Punjabi festival celebrated primarily by Sikhs and Hindus, marking the end of winter and the onset of longer days.

Goa Festival

(Goa):Goa hosts various festivals throughout the year, including the popular Goa Carnival known for its colorful parades and lively celebrations.

Vijayadashami

(Himachal Pradesh):Vijayadashami, also known as Dussehra, symbolizes the victory of good over evil and is celebrated with grand processions and theatrical performances.

Navratri

(Gujarat):- Navratri is a nine-night Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga, featuring vibrant dance performances like Garba and Dandiya.

Ganesh Chaturthi

(Maharashtra): - Ganesh Chaturthi honors the elephant-headed deity Ganesha and is celebrated with great fervor, including the installation of elaborate Ganesha idols and communal feasting.

Baisakhi

(Punjab):- Baisakhi marks the Sikh New Year and commemorates the formation of the Khalsa Panth by Guru Gobind Singh Ji, celebrated with processions, music, and feasting.

Pongal

(Tamil Nadu):- Pongal is a Tamil harvest festival celebrated with traditional rituals, including the boiling of milk and rice to honor the sun god and express gratitude for a bountiful harvest.

Dasara

(Karnataka):- Dasara, also known as Dussehra, is a Hindu festival celebrated across India, but in Karnataka, it includes the renowned Mysuru Dasara procession and festivities.

Bathukamma

(Telangana):Bathukamma is a floral festival celebrated predominantly by the women of Telangana, featuring the arrangement of colorful flowers in beautiful stacks as offerings to the goddess Bathukamma.
